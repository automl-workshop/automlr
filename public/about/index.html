Why this workshop

Research is becoming autonomous

The scientific artifact may be produced by an agent. Scientific responsibility, evaluation, and community remain human concerns.

MissionQuestionsFormatFAQ

Mission

Bring the change into the open.

Autonomous machine learning research is moving from a speculative idea to a genuine reality. Given the development of agent harnesses that can pursue long-horizon tasks, we believe it is time for the community to formally recognize and plan for the inevitability of impactful autonomous research.

Our goal is to make autonomous research meaningful in a way that strengthens the ML community without undermining the role of human researchers. The workshop creates a place to discuss autonomous research openly, instead of letting ubiquitous agent use silently redefine research norms.

To ensure conferences continue to exist for both science and human scientists, the workshop anchors autonomous research in human judgment and participation.

The intervention

Keep human judgment in the foreground.

All authors of submissions are, in an important sense, reviewers of AI-generated research. The discussant format makes that role visible.

The human author remains responsible for the paper and presents a brief summary. A human reviewer then joins as discussant to explain why the work matters, what remains uncertain, and which claims deserve the community's attention.

This design assigns visible credit for thoughtful evaluation and judgment—essential both for scientific excellence and for maintaining a sense of research community.

FAQ

Common questions

What counts as autonomous ML research?

ML research substantially produced by an autonomous agent—ideally work where an agent carried out the process end-to-end or was responsible for the central claims, including hypothesis generation and experimental or theoretical validation.

Can humans still be authors?

Yes. Authorship remains exclusively human. Authors curate the work, verify its claims, disclose agent involvement, and remain responsible for the final submission.

Can I submit work about autonomous research systems?

Yes. We welcome papers on systems that produce autonomous research, including environments, harnesses, evaluation methods, replication infrastructure, and governance.

Is the workshop archival?

No. This is a non-archival workshop, allowing authors to continue developing and submitting their work elsewhere, subject to those venues' policies.

Why do submissions nominate reviewers?

Autonomous systems lower the cost of generating papers, while evaluation remains scarce. Reviewer nominations let evaluation capacity scale with submissions and treat review labor as part of the cost of submitting autonomous research.

The point is not less humanity. It is more deliberate humanity.
